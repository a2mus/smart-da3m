# Design Context: Ihsane (إحسان)

## 1. Users
- **Primary Users (Students)**: Algerian primary students (ages 6–11). They need an emotionally safe, reassuring interface with clear micro-interactions, audio-heavy support, and age-appropriate visual hierarchy. The design must accommodate varying cognitive development stages (from icon-heavy phonological UI to structured problem-solving).
- **Secondary Users (Parents)**: Mobile-first users with varying digital literacy. They need highly scannable, clear, and reassuring data visualizations without overwhelming numbers.
- **Administrative Users (Experts)**: Pedagogical experts needing dense data management, clear CMS structures, and curriculum mapping interfaces.

## 2. Brand Personality
- **Nurturing & Reassuring**: Emotionally safe, avoiding harsh reds or strict "FAIL" states. 
- **Rooted & Authentic**: Reflecting Algerian cultural identity and Islamic values (Ihsane/Zakat al-Ilm) through subtle motifs and culturally resonant imagery.
- **Intelligent & Clear**: Professional, modern, and adaptive, instilling trust in parents and experts.

## 3. Aesthetic Direction
- **Soft Modernism**: Warm, tinted neutrals for backgrounds. Smooth exponential curves for borders. No aggressive gradients.
- **Color Palette**: 
  - Primary: Calming, trust-building colors (Soft teal or deep turquoise).
  - Secondary/Accent: Playful but grounded colors (warm yellow/ochre for achievements, soft coral for engagement).
  - Status: Soft semantic colors (mint green for success, gentle amber for review, soft rose for errors instead of stark red).
- **Typography**: 
  - Dual-language optimization: High-legibility Arabic display fonts (e.g., Cairo, Tajawal, or Ruwudu) paired with clean Latin sans-serifs (Inter or Outfit) for French/Numbers.
- **Visuals**: Clean, vector-style illustrations. Minimal use of glassmorphism; favor soft, diffuse shadows for depth and clear tactile buttons.

## 4. Design Principles
- **RTL-First Architecture**: Layouts must structurally prioritize Right-To-Left reading rhythm and eye-tracking.
- **Affective & Calm**: UI components must minimize cognitive load and frustration. Error states should feel like "learning opportunities" rather than "failures" (e.g., "Let's try a different way" vs "Wrong").
- **Touch-Optimized**: 44px minimum touch targets, 60px for early primary students.
- **Progressive Disclosure**: Dashboards (for parents/experts) should show key takeaways first, allowing drill-down into detailed analytics.

## 5. Constraints
- **Performance**: Must perform well on low-spec Android devices (Tablettes ENIE) with varying connectivity.
- **Contrast & Accessibility**: WCAG AA compliance, ensuring readability across varied screen qualities.
- **Bilingual**: UI components must gracefully expand or align based on language direction (LTR/RTL).
