# Feature Specification: Navigation Restructure & Role-Based User Experience

**Feature Branch**: `005-navigation-restructure`  
**Created**: 2026-04-18  
**Status**: Draft  
**Input**: The current site navigation is disorganized. There should be a proper welcome landing page that introduces the platform and invites visitors to sign up or sign in. After authentication, each role (Parent, Expert, Student) should be routed to its own dedicated interface. The student authenticates via their parent's email and a PIN code. Currently, the student interface is only mockups, the parent interface is incomplete, and the expert pedagogical interface is non-existent. This feature restructures the entire navigation architecture and fills in the missing dashboard experiences.

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 — Visitor Discovers the Platform and Chooses to Register or Sign In (Priority: P1)

A first-time visitor arrives on Ihsane at the root URL (`/`). They see a polished welcome landing page that clearly communicates what the platform offers: adaptive learning for Algerian primary students (ages 6–11) with personalized remediation. The page highlights key features, shows a testimonial, and presents prominent calls-to-action for two distinct paths: **Sign Up** (for new users) and **Sign In** (for returning users). The visitor understands the value proposition within 30 seconds and knows exactly how to proceed.

**Why this priority**: The landing page is the first impression and the primary entry point for all users. Without a clear, structured landing page, visitors cannot discover the platform's purpose or find how to create an account or log in. This is the foundation for all subsequent user flows.

**Independent Test**: Navigate to `/` and verify the page loads with platform description, feature highlights, and two distinct CTA buttons (Sign Up and Sign In) that route to the appropriate views.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a visitor opens the platform URL, **When** the page loads, **Then** they see a welcoming hero section with the Ihsane brand, a brief explanation of the platform's purpose in Arabic, and at least two visible CTA buttons: one for registration and one for sign-in.
2. **Given** a visitor on the landing page, **When** they click the "Sign Up" CTA, **Then** they are taken to a registration page where they can choose to register as a Parent or as an Expert.
3. **Given** a visitor on the landing page, **When** they click the "Sign In" CTA, **Then** they are taken to a sign-in page that presents role-appropriate authentication options (email/password for Parents and Experts, pseudo+PIN for Students).
4. **Given** a visitor on the landing page, **When** they scroll down, **Then** they see feature highlights, a "How It Works" section, a testimonial, and a final call-to-action section — all in Arabic (RTL layout).
5. **Given** a returning authenticated user who visits the root URL, **When** the page loads, **Then** they are automatically redirected to their role-specific dashboard.

### User Story 2 — Sign-In Routes Each Role to Its Dedicated Interface (Priority: P1)

A returning user arrives at the sign-in page. The system presents three authentication paths, each tailored to a specific role:

- **Parent**: Enters their email and password. Upon successful authentication, they are directed to the **Parent Dashboard** — a mobile-first view showing their children's progress, competency radar charts, smart messages, and pedagogical alerts.
- **Expert (Pedagogical)**: Enters their email and password. Upon successful authentication, they are directed to the **Expert Dashboard** — a desktop-oriented workspace for managing curriculum content, viewing analytics heatmaps, and monitoring student groups.
- **Student (Child)**: Enters their parent's email address and a 4-to-6-digit PIN code generated by the parent. Upon successful authentication, they are directed to the **Student Dashboard** — an age-appropriate, touch-friendly interface with their learning journey, available diagnostics, and remediation pathways.

**Why this priority**: Role-based routing is the architectural backbone of the application. Without it, no user can reach their intended experience, and all downstream features (dashboards, content creation, progress tracking) are inaccessible.

**Independent Test**: Sign in with each role type and verify redirection to the correct dashboard with role-appropriate content displayed.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a Parent on the sign-in page, **When** they enter a valid email and password, **Then** they are authenticated and redirected to `/parent` where they see their children's progress summary.
2. **Given** an Expert on the sign-in page, **When** they enter a valid email and password, **Then** they are authenticated and redirected to `/expert` where they see the content management workspace and analytics tools.
3. **Given** a Student on the sign-in page, **When** they enter their parent's email and a valid PIN code, **Then** they are authenticated and redirected to `/student` where they see their learning journey adapted to their age group.
4. **Given** any user who enters invalid credentials, **When** they submit the sign-in form, **Then** they see a clear, friendly error message in Arabic without exposing sensitive details.
5. **Given** an authenticated user at any dashboard, **When** they click "Sign Out", **Then** their session is ended and they are returned to the landing page.
6. **Given** an unauthenticated user who tries to access a dashboard URL directly (e.g., `/parent`, `/student`), **When** the page loads, **Then** they are redirected to the sign-in page with a notice.

### User Story 3 — Parent Accesses a Complete Dashboard Experience (Priority: P2)

An authenticated parent enters their dashboard and finds a comprehensive, functional interface — not a mockup. They can view each of their children's progress, explore competency radar charts, read smart pedagogical messages, review alerts about at-risk indicators, and access an analytics view with detailed breakdowns. The interface is mobile-first, uses warm nurturing tones, and communicates in plain Arabic language that non-technical parents can understand.

**Why this priority**: The parent dashboard is the primary engagement driver (target: ≥ 60% weekly access). While the current landing page and routing are prerequisites (P1), the parent experience is the most visible deliverable for pilot validation and must move beyond mockup state.

**Independent Test**: Authenticate as a parent and verify that the dashboard displays real child profiles, radar charts with data, smart messages, and navigable alert sections — all with responsive mobile layout.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** an authenticated parent, **When** they access their dashboard, **Then** they see a list of their registered children with names, grade levels, and summary progress indicators.
2. **Given** a parent viewing a child's profile, **When** they open the details, **Then** they see a radar chart showing competency mastery across at least 3 subjects.
3. **Given** a parent on the dashboard, **When** they check the alerts section, **Then** they see pedagogical alerts categorized by severity (info, warning, critical) with plain-language recommendations.
4. **Given** a parent on a smartphone (viewport < 768px), **When** they view the dashboard, **Then** all content is readable, touch targets are ≥ 48px, and no horizontal scrolling is required.
5. **Given** a parent who wants more detail, **When** they navigate to the analytics view, **Then** they see competency breakdown charts and recent activity timelines for each child.

### User Story 4 — Student Uses a Full, Age-Appropriate Learning Interface (Priority: P2)

An authenticated student enters their dashboard and sees a visually engaging, age-appropriate interface. Depending on their grade level (ages 6–7, 8–9, or 10–11), the visual complexity adapts. The interface shows their learning journey map, available diagnostic tests, current remediation pathways, and their overall progress. Touch targets are large (≥ 60px), instructions include audio support for younger students, and the interface uses warm, encouraging visuals.

**Why this priority**: The student interface is the core product experience — without it, the educational mission cannot be fulfilled. Moving from mockup to a functional interface is essential for pilot validation. However, it depends on the authentication and routing infrastructure (P1).

**Independent Test**: Authenticate as a student and verify the journey map loads with interactive nodes, progress indicators, and at least one accessible learning pathway.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** an authenticated student, **When** they enter the dashboard, **Then** they see a visual journey map showing completed, in-progress, and upcoming learning milestones.
2. **Given** a student at the dashboard, **When** they tap on an available diagnostic test, **Then** they are taken to the diagnostic session flow.
3. **Given** a student who has completed a diagnostic, **When** they return to the dashboard, **Then** they see their assigned remediation pathway with clear next steps.
4. **Given** a student aged 6–7, **When** they view the interface, **Then** the UI is icon-heavy with minimal text, large touch targets (≥ 80px), and audio instructions are available.
5. **Given** a student on a tablet (viewport 768px–1024px), **When** they interact with the journey map, **Then** nodes are pressable, transitions are smooth, and the layout fills the screen naturally.

### User Story 5 — Expert Accesses a Complete Pedagogical Management Interface (Priority: P2)

An authenticated pedagogical expert enters their dashboard and finds a full workspace for managing curriculum content and monitoring student analytics. They can navigate a curriculum tree, create and edit modules, add questions with metadata, view competency heatmaps, auto-group students by difficulty level, and drill into individual student profiles. The interface is designed for desktop and provides efficient workflows for content creation.

**Why this priority**: The expert interface is currently non-existent. Without it, pedagogical experts — who are essential for populating the platform with educational content — cannot use the system. This blocks pilot content preparation.

**Independent Test**: Authenticate as an expert and verify that the dashboard provides access to content management (module list, editor) and analytics (heatmap, student grouping).

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** an authenticated expert, **When** they enter the dashboard, **Then** they see an overview with key statistics (total modules, students, pending alerts) and quick-action links to content management and analytics.
2. **Given** an expert navigating content management, **When** they open the modules list, **Then** they see all their created modules with status indicators (draft, published) and can create a new module.
3. **Given** an expert using the analytics tools, **When** they open the competency heatmap, **Then** they see student performance data visualized across competencies with color-coded mastery levels.
4. **Given** an expert using auto-grouping, **When** they trigger the grouping feature, **Then** students are organized by difficulty level with recommended remediation strategies per group.
5. **Given** an expert on a desktop screen (viewport > 1024px), **When** they use the interface, **Then** the layout uses a sidebar-plus-content pattern that maximizes workspace efficiency.

---

### User Story 6 — New User Registers as a Parent and Sets Up Their Child (Priority: P3)

A new visitor decides to register. They choose the "Parent" registration path and create an account with their email, password, and display name (pseudo). After successful registration, they are guided through a short onboarding flow to add their first child — entering the child's name, grade level, and optionally selecting initial subjects. The system generates a unique PIN code that the parent shares with their child for student authentication.

**Why this priority**: Registration is necessary for real users to join the platform, but the pilot can initially onboard users manually. This story is important but not blocking for the initial navigation restructure.

**Independent Test**: Complete the registration flow as a parent, add a child, and verify a PIN code is generated for the child.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a visitor on the registration page, **When** they select "Parent" and fill in email, password, and display name, **Then** their account is created and they are signed in automatically.
2. **Given** a newly registered parent, **When** they complete the onboarding, **Then** they are prompted to add at least one child with name and grade level.
3. **Given** a parent adding a child, **When** the child profile is saved, **Then** the system generates a 4-to-6-digit PIN code and displays it with clear instructions to share with the child.
4. **Given** a parent who has completed registration and child setup, **When** the onboarding ends, **Then** they are redirected to their dashboard showing the newly added child.

### Edge Cases

- What happens if a student enters a valid parent email but wrong PIN? → The system shows a friendly error message ("The code is incorrect, try again or ask your parent") with a maximum of 5 attempts before a 15-minute lockout.
- What happens if a child forgets their PIN? → The parent regenerates a new PIN from the Parent Dashboard; the old PIN is invalidated immediately and the new PIN is displayed to the parent.
- What happens if a parent forgets their password? → A "Forgot Password" link on the sign-in page triggers a password reset flow via email.
- What happens if a student's parent account is deactivated? → The student's PIN becomes invalid and the child sees a message to contact their parent.
- What happens on very slow connections (< 1 Mbps)? → The landing page loads its critical content (hero, CTA buttons) within 3 seconds; secondary content loads progressively.
- What happens if navigation elements don't fit a small mobile screen? → A hamburger menu collapses the navigation links while keeping the primary CTA visible.
- What happens if the expert tries to access parent routes or vice versa? → The system redirects unauthorized role access to the user's own dashboard with an inline notification.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

#### Navigation & Page Structure

- **FR-001**: System MUST serve a dedicated public landing page at the root URL (`/`) that communicates the platform's value proposition, features, and calls-to-action for sign-up and sign-in.
- **FR-002**: System MUST provide separate, clearly distinguished paths for "Sign Up" (new users) and "Sign In" (returning users) — accessible from the landing page.
- **FR-003**: The landing page MUST include: hero section with branding, feature highlights section, "How It Works" explainer, a social-proof testimonial, and a final CTA section.
- **FR-004**: System MUST provide a unified sign-in page that first presents a role picker (Parent, Expert, Student) as visual cards. Upon selecting a role, the system reveals the role-specific authentication form: email+password for Parent or Expert, parent's email+PIN for Student. The user can switch roles without reloading the page.
- **FR-005**: System MUST redirect authenticated users away from the landing page and sign-in page directly to their role-specific dashboard.

#### Role-Based Authentication & Routing

- **FR-010**: System MUST authenticate Parents using email and password, then route them to the Parent Dashboard at `/parent`.
- **FR-011**: System MUST authenticate Experts using email and password, then route them to the Expert Dashboard at `/expert`.
- **FR-012**: System MUST authenticate Students using their parent's email address and a 4-to-6-digit PIN code, then route them to the Student Dashboard at `/student`. The parent's email serves as the unique account identifier, avoiding ambiguity from non-unique display names.
- **FR-013**: System MUST enforce route guards that prevent unauthenticated access to any dashboard and redirect to the sign-in page.
- **FR-014**: System MUST enforce role-based access control — a Parent cannot access Expert routes, a Student cannot access Parent routes, etc. Unauthorized role access redirects to the user's own dashboard.
- **FR-015**: System MUST provide a sign-out action from every dashboard that clears the session and returns the user to the landing page.

#### Parent Dashboard

- **FR-020**: The Parent Dashboard MUST display a list of the parent's registered children with name, grade level, and progress summary.
- **FR-021**: The Parent Dashboard MUST show a competency radar chart for each child covering at least 3 subjects.
- **FR-022**: The Parent Dashboard MUST display pedagogical alerts categorized by severity (info, warning, critical) with human-readable recommendations in Arabic.
- **FR-023**: The Parent Dashboard MUST provide navigation to a detailed analytics view showing competency breakdowns and activity timelines.
- **FR-024**: The Parent Dashboard MUST be mobile-first and fully functional on screens as narrow as 320px.

#### Student Dashboard

- **FR-030**: The Student Dashboard MUST display an interactive visual learning journey map showing completed, in-progress, and upcoming milestones.
- **FR-031**: The Student Dashboard MUST provide access to available diagnostic tests and remediation pathways.
- **FR-032**: The Student Dashboard MUST adapt its visual complexity based on the student's grade level: icon-heavy for ages 6–7, moderately text-rich for ages 8–9, and standard for ages 10–11.
- **FR-033**: The Student Dashboard MUST use touch targets of at least 60px for all interactive elements (80px for ages 6–7).
- **FR-034**: The Student Dashboard MUST display the student's current mastery level and progress toward the next milestone.

#### Expert Dashboard

- **FR-040**: The Expert Dashboard MUST provide an overview with key statistics: total modules created, total students monitored, and pending alerts count.
- **FR-041**: The Expert Dashboard MUST provide navigation to the content management section (module list, module editor) and analytics section (heatmap, student grouping).
- **FR-042**: The Expert Dashboard MUST display a competency heatmap visualization showing student performance across competencies.
- **FR-043**: The Expert Dashboard MUST provide auto-grouping functionality that organizes students by difficulty level.
- **FR-044**: The Expert Dashboard MUST be optimized for desktop with a sidebar navigation pattern.

#### Registration & Onboarding

- **FR-050**: System MUST provide a registration flow for Parents that collects email, password, and display name (pseudo).
- **FR-051**: System MUST guide newly registered Parents through an onboarding flow to add at least one child (name, grade level).
- **FR-052**: System MUST generate a unique 4-to-6-digit PIN code for each child added by a parent.
- **FR-053**: System MUST display the generated PIN code to the parent with clear instructions to share it with the child.
- **FR-054**: System MUST allow Parents to regenerate a child's PIN code at any time from their dashboard. The previous PIN is invalidated immediately upon regeneration, and the new PIN is displayed to the parent.

### Key Entities

- **Landing Page**: The public-facing entry point of the platform. Contains marketing content, feature descriptions, and navigation CTAs. Accessible to unauthenticated visitors only (authenticated users are redirected).
- **Sign-In Page**: A unified authentication screen presenting three role-specific login paths. Routes successful authentication to the corresponding dashboard.
- **Registration Page**: A guided sign-up flow for new Parent and Expert accounts. Includes onboarding steps for Parents to add children and receive PIN codes.
- **Parent Dashboard**: The primary interface for parents. Shows children's progress, radar charts, alerts, and analytics. Mobile-first design.
- **Student Dashboard**: The primary interface for students. Shows learning journey, diagnostics, and remediation. Age-adapted visual complexity with large touch targets.
- **Expert Dashboard**: The primary interface for pedagogical experts. Shows content management tools, analytics heatmaps, and student grouping. Desktop-optimized.
- **Child Profile**: A student record linked to a parent account via the parent's email, containing name, grade level, PIN code, and competency data.

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

### Measurable Outcomes

- **SC-001**: 100% of visitor journeys from landing page to sign-in complete without dead-end pages or broken routes.
- **SC-002**: Users can complete the sign-in flow (landing → sign-in → dashboard) in under 10 seconds for returning users.
- **SC-003**: Each role (Parent, Expert, Student) reaches a distinct, functional dashboard — not a placeholder or mockup.
- **SC-004**: The Parent Dashboard renders on mobile (320px width) with no horizontal scroll, ≥ 48px touch targets, and readable Arabic text.
- **SC-005**: The Student Dashboard renders on tablet (768px width) with ≥ 60px interactive touch targets and a visible learning journey map.
- **SC-006**: The Expert Dashboard renders on desktop (1280px width) with sidebar navigation and accessible content management tools.
- **SC-007**: Unauthorized direct URL access to any dashboard redirects to sign-in with 0% of pages showing blank or error states.
- **SC-008**: The landing page's critical content (hero + CTA) loads within 3 seconds on a standard connection.
- **SC-009**: 100% of navigation paths documented in this spec are functional and reachable — no orphaned routes or unreachable views.
- **SC-010**: New parent registration including child setup and PIN generation completes in under 5 minutes.

## Assumptions

- The current Stitch mockup routes (`/parent-dashboard`, `/parent/analytics`, `/student/journey`) will be consolidated into the proper role-based routes (`/parent`, `/parent/analytics`, `/student`). The old Stitch routes will be removed or redirected.
- The existing `Home.vue` (currently commented out in the router) and `LandingPage.vue` will be merged into a single, authoritative landing page component at `/`.
- The backend authentication API (JWT-based) is already implemented and supports login for Parents, Experts, and Students. This feature focuses on structuring the frontend navigation and building complete dashboard UIs.
- The Pinia-based `useAuthStore` already exists and handles JWT tokens, role detection, and session management. This feature extends it (if needed) to support student pseudo+PIN authentication.
- The `Login.vue` view already exists with a basic structure that separates parent/expert and student authentication — this will be enhanced, not rewritten from scratch.
- Mock data will be used for dashboard content during the pilot phase until the backend content APIs are fully populated with real data.
- Spec `004-mock-auth-state` (Mock Authentication Flow & UI State Architecture) is superseded by this spec. The mock auth route (`/mock-auth`), `useMockUiStore`, and related temporary scaffolding will be removed. The real authentication flow defined here replaces the mock role-selection approach entirely.
- The design follows the established "Nurturing Soft Modernism" design constitution (warm tones, rounded cards, RTL-first, bilingual Arabic/French support).
- Expert registration is handled separately (admin invitation or self-registration with verification) — not part of the public sign-up flow for this feature.
